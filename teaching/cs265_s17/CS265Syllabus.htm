Course Description

Introduction to the theoretical and pragmatic principles and practices of computer networking. We focus on the Internet, especially the TCP/IP protocol stack, the world-wide-web, Internet standards, and cybersecurity.

Prerequisites: CS110 and CS124. Note that these are recommendations and may be waived at instructor's discretion.

Course Materials

We will use the textbook Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach (6th Edition) by Kurose and Ross. Purchase of the textbook provides access to a companion website containing all course materials, including powerpoint slides, programming assignment descriptions and templates, and other resources such as Applets that illustrate core concepts. Purchase of this textbook is mandatory and other texts, including earlier editions of this textbook, will not be supported

Grading Policy

Final grades will be assigned based on several performance factors. These factors and their quantitative contribution to the final grade are as follows:

  • Homework and programming assigments: 70%, broken down as follows--
    • Problem sets (Biweekly): 40%
    • Major programming assignments (3 total): 30%
  • In-class exams: 30%, broken down as follows--
    • Midterm: 15%
    • Final: 15%

Attendance

Class attendance is mandatory. Attendance will be checked. More than three unexcused attendances will result in a 10% reduction of the final grade.

Exams

Two exams will be conducted during the semester, a midterm and a final. The schedule will be announced in class and content and format will be discussed prior to the exams. Make up exams will only be scheduled in emergency situations.

Homework Guidelines

Release and Submission: Both written and programming assignments will be released on the course Blackboard page with a clearly indicated due date. Solutions may be submitted electronically via Blackboard, or in class on pencil-and-paper when appropriate. Submissions are accepted until 11:59PM on the due date.

Late policy: assignments will be accepted up to seven days late, with a 10 point per day penalty. All assignments will be considered individual efforts unless otherwise specified, and will be treated as such under the academic honesty policy (see below).

Academic Integrity

We rigorously enforce the University's policy and procedures as stated in the Code of Academic Integrity. At the first suspicion of violation of this code, the case will be immediately forwarded to the University Coordinator of Academic Integrity.
  • ALL work is to be an individual effort by default. Group assignments will be explicitly stated as being so.
  • Although true research into course topics is encouraged, the course textbook and class notes are the only sanctioned sources of information for homework and exam solutions. In particular, solution sets obtained via unsanctioned means-- from friends, from online, or from taking the course previously-- are not sanctioned and any discovered use of them will be considered a violation of academic integrity.
  • The instructor is available for outside help, during office hours, by email, and by appointment.
  • It is your responsibility to protect your own work.
  • All work that is suspected to violate the Code of Academic Integrity will be referred directly to the Coordinator of Academic Integrity in the Provost's Office.
